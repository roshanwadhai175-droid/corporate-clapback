CORPORATE COMMUNICATION AND CONDUCT POLICY

Professional Tone: All communication must remain strictly professional, courteous, and constructive. Personal attacks, excessive emotional expression, and unprofessional slang are strictly prohibited.

Escalation Protocol: If an agreement cannot be reached, do not engage in repetitive arguments. Escalate the matter to your direct manager or the Human Resources department.

Respectful Disagreement: You may disagree with colleagues, but you must do so respectfully, acknowledging their points before stating your counter-arguments. Never use derogatory language.

Timely Responses: Acknowledge emails within 24 hours. If an immediate resolution is not possible, provide an estimated timeline. Ignoring emails is a violation of policy.

Work-Life Balance: Do not expect immediate responses to emails sent outside of regular business hours (9 AM to 5 PM, local time). Respect your colleagues' personal time.

Accountability: Blaming others publicly is unprofessional. Focus on finding solutions rather than pointing fingers in group communications.
